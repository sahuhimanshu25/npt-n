## Key Terminology

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**LIWC**|Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count – a tool that categorizes text into psychological and linguistic dimensions|
|**N-gram**|A sequence of N words analyzed together; unigrams (1 word), bigrams (2 words)|
|**K-means Clustering**|An unsupervised ML algorithm that groups similar data points into K clusters|
|**Valence**|The overall positivity or negativity of expressed sentiment|
|**Arousal**|The emotional intensity or activation level of expressed sentiment|
|**Communicative Policing**|A policing model emphasizing two-way, empathetic communication with the community|
|**Community Sensing**|Using social media as a real-time sensor of public mood, safety concerns, and expectations|
|**Closing the Loop**|When police provide a solution or acknowledgment that ends further discussion on a thread|
|**PO**|Police Officer / Police-initiated post|
|**C**|Citizen-initiated post|

## Detailed Notes

### 1. Research Objective & Core Question

> **Core Research Question:** Can we explore the feasibility of social media in quantifying attributes of communication (citizen-to-citizen, police-to-citizen, citizen-to-police, and police-to-police) on official police Facebook pages?

#### Four Research Dimensions

```
1. Topical Characteristics    →  What people talk about
2. Engagement Patterns        →  How actively they interact
3. Emotional Exchange         →  Positive/negative feelings and intensity
4. Cognitive & Social Orientation  →  How people think and relate linguistically
```

---

### 2. Data Collection Framework

#### Dataset Overview

|Parameter|Value|
|---|---|
|Number of police pages|**85 official Indian police Facebook pages**|
|Time span|**2010 – April 2015**|
|Total wall posts|**47,474**|
|Post distinction|PO-initiated vs C-initiated|

#### Why the PO vs C Distinction Matters

```
Police-initiated  →  Planned campaigns, institutional tone
                      → Information flows outward
Citizen-initiated →  Reactive, diverse, problem-driven
                      → Information flows inward (complaints, requests)
```

---

### 3. Topical Characteristics

#### Tools Used

```
N-gram Analysis
    ├── Unigrams  →  Single word frequency (e.g., "traffic", "safety")
    └── Bigrams   →  Word pairs (e.g., "missing person", "drunk driving")

K-means Clustering
    └── Groups posts around similar topics
```

#### Comparison: Police vs Citizen Posts

|Dimension|Police-Initiated|Citizen-Initiated|
|---|---|---|
|**Focus**|Narrow, institutional|Broad, diverse|
|**Topics**|Safety campaigns, advisories, procedural updates|Complaints, appreciation, missing persons, neighborhood issues|
|**Nature**|Planned|Reactive|

> **Key Insight:** Police posts are pre-planned campaigns; citizens use the platform primarily to seek help or voice concerns.

---

### 4. Engagement Characteristics

#### Key Findings

|Scenario|Observation|Reason|
|---|---|---|
|Police-initiated posts|Higher total interaction|Wider official reach|
|Police reply to citizen post|Fewer follow-up comments|"Closes the loop" – provides resolution|
|Citizen-only threads|More back-and-forth|Discussion stays open without official closure|

#### The "Closing the Loop" Effect

```
Citizen Post (complaint/query)
        ↓
Police Reply (solution / acknowledgment / direction)
        ↓
Discussion ends efficiently
→ Lower comment count, but resolved
```

> ⚠️ **Implication:** Official intervention resolves conversations efficiently but may limit deeper community discussion.

---

### 5. Emotional Exchange

#### Tool: LIWC (Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count)

> Categorizes text into psychological dimensions automatically.

|Sentiment Variable|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Valence**|Overall positivity (+) or negativity (−) of text|
|**Arousal**|Emotional intensity / activation level|

#### Key Findings

|Thread Type|Negative Affect|Anger|Arousal|Anxiety (after police reply)|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|Citizen-initiated|**Higher**|**Higher**|**Higher**|**Decreases**|
|Police-initiated|Lower|Lower|Lower|N/A|

> **Key Insight:** When police actively engage in citizen threads, anxiety levels **decrease** — visible official attention provides reassurance and closure.

---

### 6. Cognitive & Social Orientation

#### Pronoun Analysis

|Group|Pronoun Usage|Interpretation|
|---|---|---|
|**Citizens**|High use of _"I"_|Self-oriented; personal experiences|
|**Police**|High use of _"we"_|Collective, procedural language|

---

### 7. Why This Matters – Theoretical Implications

#### From Reactive → Proactive Policing

```
Traditional Policing  →  React to incidents after they occur
Community Sensing     →  Monitor social media as a real-time public mood sensor
Predictive Analytics  →  Anticipate emerging issues before they escalate
```

#### Practical Applications

|Application|Benefit|
|---|---|
|**Auto-flag high-priority posts**|Faster response to actionable complaints|
|**Sentiment trend tracking**|Measure changes in public trust over time|
|**Response template suggestions**|Improve consistency and efficiency|
|**Hotspot prediction**|Better resource allocation|
|**Early warning system**|Detect emerging public safety issues|
