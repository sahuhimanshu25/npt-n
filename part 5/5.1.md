2. Location Privacy (Foursquare Study)

Foursquare is a Location-Based Social Network (LBSN).
Key features:
Check-ins: User marks presence at a venue.
Tips: Reviews/comments left at locations.
Mayorship: Awarded to the person with the most check-ins at a venue in last 60 days (gamification).


Findings:

Human mobility is highly regular and predictable.
Home location can be inferred with high accuracy (within a few km) using check-in patterns and mayorship data.
Even without direct home address, repeated patterns reveal sensitive location information.

Broader Implication: Self-disclosure (check-ins, location-tagged photos) + advanced inference techniques seriously threaten privacy.
3. Policing on Online Social Media
Historical Milestone:

2009 US Airways Flight 1549 (Hudson River landing) — First major real-world crisis where citizens used Twitter to report live before official responders arrived. Marked the shift of social media from personal chatter to crisis communication tool.

Police Strategies:

Community engagement via hashtags (e.g., #myNYPD, #myLAPD) — intended for positive photos but often backfired with criticism.
Traffic updates, crime alerts, appreciation posts, rewards for information, public announcements.

Indian Context:

Major adopters: Bangalore City Police, Delhi Traffic Police, Hyderabad City Police, etc.
Platforms: Facebook (high likes/engagement) and Twitter (real-time updates).
Most pages allow public posting/comments.
Verified Accounts (Blue Tick): Critical for authenticity. Only a small fraction of accounts are verified.

Major Challenge – Masquerading / Fake Accounts:

Many fake handles impersonating police departments.
Difficult for citizens to distinguish real vs fake.
Course instructor created a tracking page listing legitimate state/city police handles with verification status and official website links.

4. Technical Analysis of Police Social Media

Data collected via APIs → Stored in databases (MySQL/MongoDB).
Analysis performed:
Temporal (when they post).
Engagement metrics (likes, comments, shares).
Content types (traffic, alerts, appreciation).


Students now have the skills (Python + Twython + Databases) to perform such analysis.
