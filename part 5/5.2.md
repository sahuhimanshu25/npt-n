## Key Terminology

| Term                       | Definition                                                                                                          |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Actionable Information** | Specific, time-sensitive, location-aware data that enables immediate police response                                |
| **Re-identification**      | Linking anonymous/unidentified data with identified sources to uniquely identify a person                           |
| **Word Tree**              | A visualization technique for exploring recurring linguistic patterns and themes in text                            |
| **Communicative Policing** | A model of policing that emphasizes two-way communication and community engagement                                  |
| **Mutual Accountability**  | The idea that a public platform holds both police (for responses) and citizens (for accurate reporting) accountable |
| **Direct Information**     | A personal complaint filed by the affected individual                                                               |
| **Indirect Information**   | Reporting a complaint on behalf of others or the neighborhood                                                       |
|                            |                                                                                                                     |

## Detailed Notes

### 1. Research Objective

Can Online Social Media (especially Facebook & Twitter) help police:

- Get **actionable information** (location + time + specific problem)?
- Understand **residents' opinions and sentiment**?

#### Examples of Actionable Information

```
✅ "Pothole at XYZ junction"
✅ "Car broken down blocking traffic at ABC road at 5:30 PM"
✅ "Illegal parking / auto driver issue at MG Road"
```

### 2. Re-identification Recap

> ⚠️ **Important Concept for Exam**

```
Unidentified Data (e.g., dating site pseudonym)
            +
Identified Data (e.g., Facebook profiles)
            ↓
    Unique Identification of Individual
```

**Classic Example – Latanya Sweeney's Work:**

|Data Combined|Fields Used|
|---|---|
|Medical records + Voter lists|Zip code + Gender + Date of Birth|
|Result|87% of the US population uniquely identifiable|

### 3. Methodology – Bangalore City Police Study (2014)

#### Dataset

|Element|Count|
|---|---|
|Posts analyzed|**255**|
|Comments analyzed|**~1,600**|
|Source|BCP Facebook page|

#### Analysis Dimensions

```
Content Analysis
    ├── 24 categories (traffic, fraud, neighborhood concerns, appreciation, etc.)
    ├── Style: Formal vs Informal
    └── Police Response: Acknowledge / Reply / Follow-up / Ignore

Geospatial Analysis
    └── Heatmaps of complaint locations → identify hotspots

Temporal Analysis
    └── Response time distribution (4 min – 211 hours)
```

#### Key Results

|Finding|Detail|
|---|---|
|**Most common citizen posts**|Neighborhood concerns, Appreciation, Suggestions, Traffic issues|
|**Highest likes**|Appreciation posts & success stories|
|**Police response style**|Mostly **formal**|
|**Avg. response time**|~30 hours (high variance)|

---

### 4. Advanced Text Analysis Techniques

#### Word Trees

> Visualize recurring phrases anchored to a key word.

|Keyword|Reveals|
|---|---|
|_"worried about"_|Citizen anxieties and fears|
|_"need to"_|Citizen requirements and demands|
|_"want to"_|Citizen expectations|

#### Direct vs Indirect Information

|Type|Description|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Direct**|Personal complaint by the affected person|_"My bike was stolen"_|
|**Indirect**|Reporting on behalf of others/neighborhood|_"There's a pothole near our colony"_|

#### Mutual Accountability

```
Public Social Media Platform
        ↓
Police are accountable  →  Must respond to visible complaints
Citizens are accountable →  Must report accurately (posts are public)
```

### Additional High-Probability MCQs (11–30)

**Q11.** The main goal of the Bangalore study was to check if social media can provide ________ information to police.

> **Ans:** Actionable

**Q12.** True/False: Response time variance shows uniform efficiency across all complaints.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – there is high variance (4 min to 211 hours)

**Q13.** In word tree analysis, words like "worried", "need", and "want" help extract citizen ________.

> **Ans:** Needs / anxieties / expectations

**Q14.** One major challenge in police social media is ________ accounts.

> **Ans:** Fake / masquerading

**Q15.** True/False: All police responses were informal.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – most responses were **formal**

**Q16.** The re-identification experiment using campus photos vs Facebook achieved ________ % success.

> **Ans:** **38%**

**Q17.** Online vs Online re-identification (dating site + Facebook) achieved approximately ________ % success.

> **Ans:** **10%**

**Q18.** The SSN inference attack achieved ________ % success with only 4 attempts.

> **Ans:** **27%**

**Q19.** Foursquare's Mayorship is awarded based on the most check-ins in the last ________ days.

> **Ans:** **60 days**

**Q20.** True/False: JSON is the data format used when collecting data from social media APIs.

> **Ans:** ✅ **True**

**Q21.** True/False: Appreciation posts received fewer likes than traffic complaint posts.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – appreciation and success stories received **higher** likes

**Q22.** Fill in the blank: Heatmaps generated from geotagged posts help police identify complaint ________.

> **Ans:** Hotspots

**Q23.** What does "communicative policing" emphasize?

> **Ans:** Two-way communication and community engagement between police and citizens

**Q24.** Scenario: A citizen posts "There's a stolen car parked on MG Road since yesterday morning." Is this direct or indirect information? Is it actionable?

> **Ans:** **Indirect** (reporting on behalf of neighborhood); **Yes, it is actionable** – it has a location and is time-sensitive.

**Q25.** Scenario: Police want to reduce their average response time of 30 hours. Based on the study, what approach would help?

> **Ans:** Real-time analysis to flag high-priority posts; better resource allocation guided by hotspot and temporal analysis.

**Q26.** True/False: Geospatial analysis can reveal which areas generate the most complaints.

> **Ans:** ✅ **True**

**Q27.** True/False: The Bangalore City Police study used Twitter data as its primary source.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – it used **Facebook** page data

**Q28.** What year was the Bangalore City Police data collected?

> **Ans:** **2014**

**Q29.** True/False: Word Trees are a type of geospatial analysis tool.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – Word Trees are a **text/linguistic analysis** tool

**Q30.** Scenario: You collect data from your city's police Facebook page. What analyses can you perform?

> **Ans:** Content categorization (24 categories), temporal analysis (when posts occur), engagement metrics (likes, comments), geospatial hotspot mapping, word tree analysis for themes, and police response rate/latency analysis.
