## Overview

This module explores **Anonymous Social Networks (ASNs)** — platforms that prioritize user anonymity over persistent identity. Unlike traditional Online Social Networks (Facebook, Twitter), ASNs allow users to share content without clear attribution.

**Examples of ASNs:** 4chan · Whisper · Secret · Yik Yak · Wickr

---

## Why Anonymous Networks?

- Growing **privacy awareness** among users
- Historical surveillance concerns (Snowden / PRISM revelations)
- Real-world consequences from public posting on identity-driven platforms
- Desire for uninhibited self-expression

---

## Whisper — Case Study

Whisper is a **confessional app** where users post thoughts, secrets, and memes anonymously.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Content format | Text overlaid on auto-generated images (memes) |
| Interactions | Hearts (likes) and private chats |
| Identity storage | No persistent user identities stored |
| Authentication | PIN-based access (no passwords) |

### Research Dataset (2014)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Whispers collected | ~9 million |
| Replies collected | ~15 million |
| GUIDs collected | ~1 million |

Researchers later collaborated with Whisper to **remove GUIDs**, enhancing anonymity.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Interaction Dynamics

- **55%** of posts receive **zero replies**
- Posts that don't gain traction within **1 hour** are unlikely to later
- Engagement follows a **power-law distribution** — a small percentage of users drive most activity

### 2. Network Structure

Whisper behaves as a **Random Graph**, not a Small-World network:

| Metric | Whisper | Facebook/Twitter |
|---|---|---|
| Average degree | High | High |
| Clustering coefficient | **Low** | High |
| Assortativity coefficient | ~0 (random) | Positive (hubs/clusters) |
| Network type | Random graph | Small-world |

> **Implication:** Interactions occur mostly with strangers rather than tight social circles.

### 3. Content Deletion & Moderation

- **18%** of Whisper content is deleted (vs. **4%** on Twitter)
- Peak deletion window: **3–9 hours** after posting
- Highest deletion categories: sexting, selfies, casual chat
- Reflects **user regret** and/or platform moderation of sensitive content

### 4. Geo-Spatial Dynamics

- **90%** of interacting user pairs are in the **same state**
- **75%** are within **40 miles** of each other
- Smaller local populations → higher interaction likelihood

### 5. Engagement Over Time

- Despite a growing user base, daily new posts remain **stable**
- Indicates high **churn** — users join, post briefly, then go inactive
- **Stickiness** (long-term retention) is low compared to identity-driven platforms

## Key Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **ASN** | Anonymous Social Network — platform where user identity is hidden |
| **Ephemeral Social Network** | Network where content and connections are transient |
| **GUID** | Global User Identifier — unique ID assigned to each user |
| **Clustering Coefficient** | Measure of how tightly a user's contacts are interconnected |
| **Assortativity Coefficient** | Tendency of nodes to connect with similar-degree nodes |
| **Stickiness** | Long-term user retention and engagement on a platform |
| **Power-law Distribution** | Pattern where few items have high frequency, most have low |

---

## Overall Implications

| Aspect | Impact of Anonymity |
|---|---|
| Expression | Freer, more uninhibited |
| Interactions | Transient and random |
| Content deletion | Significantly higher |
| Social ties | Weak, non-persistent |
| Community formation | Difficult |
| User retention | Low (high churn) |
| Trust & accountability | Reduced |
