## Key Terminology

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Phishing**|Tricking users into revealing credentials via fake/malicious links or pages|
|**Social Engineering**|Psychological manipulation exploiting trust, urgency, greed, or curiosity rather than technical vulnerabilities|
|**Hashtag Hijacking**|Co-opting trending hashtags to inject spam, phishing, or irrelevant content|
|**Clickbait**|Sensational or misleading headlines designed to drive clicks to malicious or low-quality sites|
|**Epistemic Uncertainty**|Difficulty in determining what content or accounts are genuine/trustworthy due to prevalence of fakes|
|**Spear Phishing**|A highly targeted form of phishing aimed at a specific individual or organization|
|**FOMO**|Fear Of Missing Out – psychological trigger exploited by fake live streaming and trending event scams|
|**Social Proof**|The psychological tendency to trust something that others seem to approve of (likes, reviews, shares)|
|**Social Reputation Manipulation**|Artificially inflating likes, reviews, or endorsements to deceive algorithms and users|

#### Why Social Media Is Different from Traditional Cybercrime

|Traditional Cybercrime|OSM eCrime|
|---|---|
|Targets technical vulnerabilities|Targets human psychology|
|Often isolated attacks|Exploits virality and network effects|
|Limited reach|Massive, instantaneous reach|
|Low trust context (email spam)|High trust context (social connections)|

### 3. Why Social Media Credentials Are High-Value

```
Traditional Bank Password
    └── Access to one account

Social Media Account
    ├── Full social graph (friends, family, colleagues)
    ├── Interests and behavioral patterns
    ├── Private messages and relationships
    ├── Connected apps and services
    └── Platform for spreading attacks to the victim's entire network
```

> ⭐ **Key Exam Point:** Social media credentials are MORE valuable than bank details because they enable highly targeted social engineering attacks.

### 4. Deep Dive: Each Crime Type

#### 🎣 Phishing

- Evolved from email-based to OSM-based
- Malicious links posted in **comments, DMs, or trending threads**
- Often impersonates official platform support
- Targets: credentials, personal data, financial info

#### 🎭 Fake Customer Service Accounts

```
Victim tweets: "@BankXYZ I can't log in to my account! Help!"
                    ↓
Attacker monitors public complaints in real time
                    ↓
Fake @BankXYZ_Support replies: "We can help! Click here..."
                    ↓
Victim, already frustrated, shares sensitive details
```

> **Why it works:** Exploits **trust** placed in responsive, official-looking handles and the victim's existing frustration.

#### 🤖 Fake Comments & Fake Live Streaming

|Type|Psychological Trigger|Mechanism|
|---|---|---|
|Fake Comments|Curiosity, social proof|Bot comments on trending posts with malicious links|
|Fake Live Streaming|FOMO, urgency|Fake live event page (Olympics, sports) redirects to phishing site|

#### 🎁 Fake Discounts, Surveys & Foursquare Tips

|Platform|Crime|Lure|
|---|---|---|
|Facebook/Instagram|Fake Discount Contests|"Win ₹10,000! Fill this quick survey"|
|Foursquare|Fake Tips|Malicious links disguised as venue recommendations|
|General|Fake Personality Surveys|Data harvesting under entertainment guise|

> **Why Foursquare Tips are dangerous:** They blend seamlessly with legitimate user-generated content at real, trusted venues.

#### 👍 Social Reputation Manipulation

```
Fake Likes / Followers
        ↓
Artificial credibility boost
        ↓
Algorithm promotes the content
        ↓
Real users trust and engage
        ↓
Fraudsters profit (sales / misinformation / competitor damage)
```

> **Core Concept:** Exploits the **attention economy** — social proof (likes, ratings, shares) heavily influences real user decisions.

#### 🔗 Clickbaiting & Hashtag Hijacking

|Crime|Algorithm Exploited|User Psychology Exploited|
|---|---|---|
|**Clickbaiting**|Engagement-based content ranking|Curiosity, sensationalism|
|**Hashtag Hijacking**|Trending topic discovery|Trust in popular conversations|

**Hashtag Hijacking Examples:**

- `#Rio2016` → injected with spam/phishing
- Disaster hashtags → scammers insert fake donation links
- Police hashtags (e.g., `#myNYPD`) → negative content hijacking

#### 🔓 Compromised Accounts & Impersonation

> **Famous Example:** AP (Associated Press) Twitter account hacked → fake tweet about White House explosion → US stock market briefly crashed.

|Type|Harm|
|---|---|
|**Compromised Verified Account**|Carries existing trust; causes widespread panic or market impact|
|**Impersonation Account**|Erodes epistemic trust; users cannot distinguish real from fake|

> **Epistemic Uncertainty:** When fake accounts and compromised verified accounts coexist, users lose the ability to trust any content — this is the deepest harm.

#### 💼 Work-from-Home Scams

- Commonly spread via **image-based posts** on platforms like **Pinterest**
- Promise easy income through surveys or simple tasks
- Outcome: **data theft or financial fraud**

---

### 5. Theoretical Framework

#### Why These Crimes Thrive on Social Media

```
Social Media Design Features
    ├── High Trust Environment  →  Users trust friends' shares
    ├── Rapid Virality          →  Attacks spread instantly
    ├── Emotional Engagement    →  Triggers override critical thinking
    └── Massive Reach           →  One post → millions of potential victims
```

#### The Social Engineering Spectrum

|Trigger|Crime Types That Exploit It|
|---|---|
|**Trust**|Fake Customer Service, Impersonation, Compromised Accounts|
|**Urgency**|Fake Live Streaming, Phishing DMs|
|**Greed**|Fake Discounts, Work-from-Home Scams, Fake Surveys|
|**Curiosity**|Clickbait, Fake Comments, Fake Live Streaming|
|**Fear / FOMO**|Fake Live Events, Disaster Hashtag Scams|

### Additional Expected MCQs (11–25)

**Q11.** Spear phishing differs from general phishing in that it is ________.

> **Ans:** Highly targeted (aimed at a specific individual or organization)

**Q12.** True/False: Hashtag hijacking is always illegal.

> **Ans:** ❌ **Not necessarily** – it is often **unethical and misleading** but legality depends on context

**Q13.** The lecture emphasizes that many eCrimes on OSM rely more on ________ than pure technical hacks.

> **Ans:** Social engineering

**Q14.** Fake live streaming scams exploit user ________ around major events.

> **Ans:** Curiosity / FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out)

**Q15.** True/False: Social media credentials are less valuable than bank passwords to an attacker.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – social media credentials are MORE valuable due to access to social graphs and behavioral data

**Q16.** Which psychological trigger does a "Win ₹10,000 – fill this survey!" post primarily exploit?

> **Ans:** **Greed**

**Q17.** True/False: Fake Customer Service scams require hacking the official company account.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – attackers create look-alike impersonation accounts; no hacking required

**Q18.** What is "social proof" and why is it dangerous in the context of eCrime?

> **Ans:** Social proof is the tendency to trust content that others approve of (likes, shares, ratings). Criminals manipulate it via fake likes/reviews to make fraudulent content appear credible.

**Q19.** Scenario: You see a trending post about a major cricket match with hundreds of comments containing a link to "watch live for free." What crime type is this likely?

> **Ans:** **Fake Live Streaming** scam exploiting FOMO and curiosity around a major sporting event.

**Q20.** Scenario: Someone tags a bank on Twitter saying they're locked out. Within minutes, an account named "@BankXYZ_Help" replies asking for details. What crime is this?

> **Ans:** **Fake Customer Service Account** – attackers monitor public complaints and respond from impersonation accounts.

**Q21.** Fill in the blank: Buying fake followers and likes is an example of ________ manipulation.

> **Ans:** Social reputation manipulation

**Q22.** True/False: Work-from-Home scams only steal money directly.

> **Ans:** ❌ **False** – they primarily lead to **data theft**, which may also result in financial fraud

**Q23.** From Week 5: The concept of Mutual Accountability means both police and citizens are publicly accountable because interactions happen on ________ platforms.

> **Ans:** Public / open social media platforms

**Q24.** From Week 5: Which tool was used to analyze psychological dimensions of police-citizen communication?

> **Ans:** **LIWC** (Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count)

**Q25.** Scenario: A scammer creates a fake page for a popular restaurant on Foursquare and posts "tips" with a link to a "special discount." What two crime types overlap here?

> **Ans:** **Fake Tips** (Foursquare-specific) + **Fake Discounts/Surveys** – both use trusted platform features to lure victims to malicious links.
